What I've Done

Although I am only a high school student, I have had the opportunity to work and volunteer at plenty of interesting and distinct places. For more detail, you can check out my résumé, or contact me below.

Starmail

My first paid job ever was working for Starmail as a Youth Carrier assembling and delivering flyer packages to select households. While I worked, I developed my first work-related skills, such as organization, repetition, efficiency, and time management. Assembling the flyers efficiently required you to organize each individual flyer so that you can quickly collect one of each flyer for a complete flyer package. As well, delivering the flyers on a set date required good time management between school, work, and other activities.

Mikutech

I first joined Mikutech as a high school coop student as part of my school's coop program. While there, I learned immense amounts of insight and knowledge into the development process of a quality piece of software. I began by testing/debugging the software for bugs, but soon partook in other activities such as programming and researching. Mikutech decided they wanted me for a paid summer position as a general programmer, and I jumped on the opportunity. From there, I delved deeper into the programming cycle and helped the other programmers develop part of a mobile game.

FLL/FRC Robotics

Ever since elementary school, I have been involved in FIRST robotics teams and competitions. I've been an original member of my elementary school's first FLL robotics team, and as a team, we have gone on to win 3 awards in the city competition as well as advance to regionals. Later, I returned as a mentor and advisor for the team. In high school, I have also dabbled in Western Engineering's FRC team, and was part of the web development group that developed the team's website.
